Constitutional Amendments

How the Constitution has evolved through landmark amendments

The Indian Constitution has been amended over 105 times since its adoption. Amendments are made under Article 368. Some amendments have fundamentally changed the nature and scope of the Constitution. Below are the most significant amendments.

AmendmentYearKey Features
1st Amendment1951Added Ninth Schedule to protect land reform laws from judicial review. Restricted freedom of speech for public order, morality, and security of State. Added Articles 31A, 31B.
7th Amendment1956Reorganization of States on a linguistic basis. Abolished classification into Parts A, B, C, and D states. Introduced two or more common High Courts for two or more states.
13th Amendment1962Formed the State of Nagaland with special provisions (Article 371A).
24th Amendment1971Affirmed Parliament's power to amend Fundamental Rights. Response to the Supreme Court's Golaknath judgment. Made it obligatory for the President to give assent to a Constitution Amendment Bill.
25th Amendment1971Curtailed the right to property. Introduced Article 31C to give primacy to DPSP (Articles 39b and 39c) over Fundamental Rights (Articles 14 and 19).
42nd Amendment ⭐1976"Mini-Constitution" — The most comprehensive amendment. Added "Socialist, Secular, and Integrity" to the Preamble. Added Fundamental Duties (Part IVA). Added Part XIVA (Tribunals). Extended term of Lok Sabha and State Assemblies to 6 years. Curtailed judicial review powers. Made DPSP supreme over Fundamental Rights. Transferred subjects from State to Concurrent List.
44th Amendment ⭐1978Reversed many changes of the 42nd Amendment. Right to Property removed as Fundamental Right (made legal right under Art. 300A). Restored term of Lok Sabha to 5 years. Restored power of judicial review. National Emergency only on "armed rebellion" (not "internal disturbance"). President can refer Cabinet advice back for reconsideration once.
52nd Amendment ⭐1985Added the Tenth Schedule (Anti-Defection Law). Members of Parliament/State Legislature are disqualified for defecting from their party. Decision by Speaker/Chairman.
61st Amendment1989Reduced voting age from 21 to 18 years for elections to the Lok Sabha and Legislative Assemblies (Article 326).
65th Amendment1990Established the National Commission for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Article 338).
73rd Amendment ⭐1992Gave constitutional status to Panchayati Raj. Added Part IX and Eleventh Schedule. Three-tier system of local governance. Reservation for women (1/3 seats). Regular elections every 5 years. State Election Commission. State Finance Commission.
74th Amendment ⭐1992Gave constitutional status to Municipalities. Added Part IXA and Twelfth Schedule. Three types of urban bodies: Nagar Panchayat, Municipal Council, Municipal Corporation. Reservation for women (1/3 seats).
84th Amendment2001Extended the freeze on delimitation of constituencies till 2026 (based on 1971 Census data).
86th Amendment ⭐2002Made Right to Education a Fundamental Right for children aged 6–14 (Article 21A). Added a corresponding Fundamental Duty 51A(k) for parents.
91st Amendment2003Modified the Anti-Defection Law. Removed the protection for splits. No disqualification if 2/3 of a legislature party merges with another party.
97th Amendment2011Gave constitutional protection to co-operative societies. Added Part IXB (Articles 243ZH to 243ZT). Made right to form co-operative societies a Fundamental Right under Article 19(1)(c).
101st Amendment ⭐2016Introduced Goods and Services Tax (GST). Added Articles 246A, 269A, 279A. Created the GST Council. Subsumed multiple indirect taxes into one unified tax system.
102nd Amendment2018Granted constitutional status to the National Commission for Backward Classes (Article 338B). Added Article 342A for specifying socially and educationally backward classes.
103rd Amendment ⭐2019Provided 10% reservation for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) in education and government employment. Added clauses to Articles 15 and 16.
104th Amendment2020Extended reservation of seats for SCs and STs in Lok Sabha and State Assemblies for another 10 years (until January 2030). Removed reserved seats for Anglo-Indian community.
105th Amendment2021Restored the power of State governments to identify their own lists of OBCs (Other Backward Classes).
