Fundamental Duties

The moral obligations of every citizen — Article 51A (Part IVA)

Fundamental Duties were added to the Constitution by the 42nd Amendment Act, 1976 on the recommendation of the Swaran Singh Committee. Originally 10 duties were added; the 11th duty was added by the 86th Amendment Act, 2002. These duties are non-justiciable (not enforceable by courts) but serve as moral reminders for citizens.

🇮🇳

51A(a) — Respect the Constitution, National Flag, and National Anthem

To abide by the Constitution and respect its ideals and institutions, the National Flag and the National Anthem.

✊

51A(b) — Cherish the noble ideals of the freedom struggle

To cherish and follow the noble ideals which inspired our national struggle for freedom.

🤝

51A(c) — Uphold the sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India

To uphold and protect the sovereignty, unity and integrity of India.

🛡️

51A(d) — Defend the country and render national service

To defend the country and render national service when called upon to do so.

🕊️

51A(e) — Promote harmony and brotherhood

To promote harmony and the spirit of common brotherhood amongst all the people of India transcending religious, linguistic and regional or sectional diversities; to renounce practices derogatory to the dignity of women.

🏛️

51A(f) — Value and preserve the rich heritage

To value and preserve the rich heritage of our composite culture.

🌿

51A(g) — Protect the natural environment

To protect and improve the natural environment including forests, lakes, rivers and wild life, and to have compassion for living creatures.

🧪

51A(h) — Develop the scientific temper

To develop the scientific temper, humanism and the spirit of inquiry and reform.

🏢

51A(i) — Safeguard public property

To safeguard public property and to abjure violence.

⭐

51A(j) — Strive towards excellence

To strive towards excellence in all spheres of individual and collective activity so that the nation constantly rises to higher levels of endeavour and achievement.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

51A(k) — Provide opportunities for education to children

A parent or guardian shall provide opportunities for education to his child or ward between the age of six and fourteen years. (Added by the 86th Amendment, 2002)
