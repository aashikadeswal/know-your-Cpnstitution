Schedules (1–12)

Additional details, lists, and provisions that supplement the Constitution's Articles

1️⃣

First Schedule

Articles 1 & 4

Purpose: Lists the States and Union Territories of India and describes their territories.

Currently lists 28 States and 8 Union Territories. Updated whenever new states are formed or UTs are reorganized (e.g., formation of Telangana in 2014, reorganization of J&K in 2019).

2️⃣

Second Schedule

Articles 59, 65, 75, 97, 125, 148, 158, 164, 186, 221

Purpose: Provisions relating to emoluments, allowances, privileges of the President, Governors, Speaker/Deputy Speaker, Chairman/Deputy Chairman, Judges of the Supreme Court and High Courts, and the CAG.

Divided into five parts covering different office holders.

3️⃣

Third Schedule

Articles 75, 84, 99, 124, 148, 164, 188, 219

Purpose: Contains the forms of oaths or affirmations.

Prescribes oaths for Ministers, Members of Parliament, Members of State Legislature, Judges of Supreme Court and High Courts, and the CAG before assuming office.

4️⃣

Fourth Schedule

Articles 4 & 80

Purpose: Allocation of seats in the Rajya Sabha (Council of States) to States and Union Territories.

Members are allocated based on population. Currently, Rajya Sabha has a maximum of 250 members — 238 elected from States/UTs and 12 nominated by the President.

5️⃣

Fifth Schedule

Article 244(1)

Purpose: Administration and control of Scheduled Areas and Scheduled Tribes in states other than the four northeastern states covered by the Sixth Schedule.

The Governor has special powers for Scheduled Areas and can modify Central and State laws in their application to such areas.

6️⃣

Sixth Schedule

Articles 244(2) & 275(1)

Purpose: Administration of tribal areas in four northeastern states: Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, and Mizoram.

Provides for Autonomous District Councils and Regional Councils with powers to make laws on land, forests, water, marriages, social customs, and village/town management.

7️⃣

Seventh Schedule ⭐

Article 246

Purpose: Division of subjects into three lists that determine legislative powers:

Union List (List I): 97 subjects. Parliament has exclusive power. Example: Defence, Atomic energy, Foreign affairs, Railways, Banking, Currency.

State List (List II): 66 subjects. State Legislature has exclusive power. Example: Police, Public health, Agriculture, Land revenue, Local government.

Concurrent List (List III): 47 subjects. Both Parliament and State can legislate. Example: Criminal law, Marriage, Education, Forests, Trade unions.

8️⃣

Eighth Schedule

Articles 344(1) & 351

Purpose: Lists the 22 official languages recognized by the Constitution.

Originally 14 languages; now 22 — Assamese, Bengali, Bodo, Dogri, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Kashmiri, Konkani, Maithili, Malayalam, Manipuri, Marathi, Nepali, Odia, Punjabi, Sanskrit, Santali, Sindhi, Tamil, Telugu, and Urdu.

9️⃣

Ninth Schedule

Article 31B

Purpose: Contains acts and regulations related to land reforms that are placed beyond judicial review.

Added by the 1st Amendment (1951) to validate land reform laws. Currently contains 284 acts. After the I.R. Coelho case (2007), acts added after April 24, 1973 are subject to judicial review if they violate the basic structure.

🔟

Tenth Schedule ⭐

Articles 102(2) & 191(2)

Purpose: Contains provisions relating to the Anti-Defection Law.

Added by the 52nd Amendment (1985). A member of Parliament or State Legislature is disqualified if they voluntarily give up party membership or vote/abstain against party direction (whip). The decision is made by the Speaker/Chairman. Modified by the 91st Amendment (2003) to remove the protection for splits.

1️⃣1️⃣

Eleventh Schedule

Article 243G

Purpose: Contains 29 subjects on which Panchayats can make laws and function.

Added by the 73rd Amendment (1992). Includes: agriculture, land improvement, minor irrigation, animal husbandry, fisheries, social forestry, small scale industries, drinking water, roads, rural housing, education, health, family welfare, women and child development, social welfare, public distribution system, maintenance of community assets.

1️⃣2️⃣

Twelfth Schedule

Article 243W

Purpose: Contains 18 subjects on which Municipalities can function and legislate.

Added by the 74th Amendment (1992). Includes: urban planning, regulation of land use, roads and bridges, water supply, public health, fire services, urban forestry, slum improvement, urban poverty alleviation, provision for disabled, burials and burial grounds, cattle pounds, public amenities, regulation of slaughter houses.
