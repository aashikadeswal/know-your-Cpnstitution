Constitutional Timeline

Tracing the journey from colonial rule to the world's largest written Constitution

1773

Regulating Act

First step by British Parliament to regulate the affairs of the East India Company. Established a Governor-General of Bengal (Warren Hastings) and a Supreme Court at Calcutta.

1833

Charter Act

Made the Governor-General of Bengal the Governor-General of India. Began the centralization of administrative power. First time the term "Government of India" was officially used.

1858

Government of India Act

After the Revolt of 1857, the British Crown assumed direct control of India from the East India Company. Created the office of Secretary of State for India and the Indian Council.

1861

Indian Councils Act

Introduced the portfolio system and allowed Indians to be associated with the law-making process as non-official members. Beginning of representative institutions in India.

1892

Indian Councils Act

Introduced the principle of elections (indirect) for the first time. Enlarged the size of legislative councils. Members could discuss the budget and ask questions to the executive.

1909

Morley-Minto Reforms

Indian Councils Act increased the size of legislative councils. Introduced separate electorates for Muslims — the beginning of communal representation. First Indian (Satyendra Sinha) appointed to the Viceroy's Executive Council.

1919

Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms

Government of India Act introduced Dyarchy (dual government) in provinces. Central subjects were divided into Reserved and Transferred. Introduced bicameralism at the Centre and direct elections.

1927

Simon Commission

All-white British commission (chaired by Sir John Simon) sent to study constitutional reform. Met with widespread boycott and protests ("Simon Go Back"). Led to the expansion of Indian participation in constitutional discussions.

1928

Nehru Report

Committee headed by Motilal Nehru drafted a constitutional framework for India. Demanded Dominion Status, fundamental rights, responsible government, and joint electorates. First Indian attempt at constitution-making.

1935

Government of India Act

The most extensive and detailed act. Proposed an All-India Federation (never realized), established provincial autonomy, introduced bicameralism in 6 provinces, and divided powers into three lists. Served as the foundation for the Indian Constitution.

1942

Cripps Mission & Quit India

Sir Stafford Cripps offered India dominion status after the war with a constitution-making body. Rejected by Congress. Mahatma Gandhi launched the Quit India Movement demanding immediate independence.

1946

Cabinet Mission & Constituent Assembly

Cabinet Mission Plan proposed the framework for the Constituent Assembly. The Assembly first met on December 9, 1946, with Dr. Sachidanand Sinha as temporary President. Dr. Rajendra Prasad was elected as permanent President.

1947

Independence & Drafting Begins

India gained independence on August 15, 1947. The Drafting Committee was formed on August 29, 1947, with Dr. B.R. Ambedkar as Chairman. The work of framing the Constitution began in earnest.

26 Nov 1949

Constitution Adopted 🇮🇳

After 2 years, 11 months, and 18 days, the Constitution was adopted by the Constituent Assembly. 284 members signed the final document. November 26 is celebrated as Constitution Day (Samvidhan Divas).

26 Jan 1950

Constitution Comes into Force 🎉

The Constitution of India came into effect, making India a Sovereign Democratic Republic. January 26 was chosen because the Indian National Congress had declared Purna Swaraj (complete independence) on January 26, 1930. Dr. Rajendra Prasad became the first President of India.

1951

First Amendment

The 1st Amendment Act added the Ninth Schedule and restricted the freedoms under Article 19 for public order and security. Validated zamindari abolition laws. First of many amendments to come.

1976

42nd Amendment — "Mini-Constitution"

The most comprehensive amendment. Added "Socialist, Secular" to the Preamble. Added Fundamental Duties. Curtailed judicial powers. Made during the Emergency period under Indira Gandhi's government.

1992

73rd & 74th Amendments

Gave constitutional status to Panchayats (73rd) and Municipalities (74th). Decentralized governance and empowered local self-government across India.

2016

101st Amendment — GST

Introduced the Goods and Services Tax, the most significant tax reform since independence. Created a unified national market by subsuming multiple indirect taxes.

Present

A Living Document

With 105+ amendments, the Constitution continues to evolve. It remains the foundation of India's democracy — adapting to modern challenges while preserving the ideals of justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity.
