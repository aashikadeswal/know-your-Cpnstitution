Parts of the Constitution

The Indian Constitution is organized into 22 Parts (currently 25 with additions)

PartTitleArticlesDescription
IThe Union and its Territory1–4Name and territory of India, admission/formation of new states.
IICitizenship5–11Provisions for citizenship at the commencement and laws relating to it.
IIIFundamental Rights12–35Six categories of rights guaranteed to all citizens, enforceable by courts.
IVDirective Principles of State Policy36–51Non-justiciable guidelines for the State in governance and policy-making.
IVAFundamental Duties51A11 duties of every citizen. Added by 42nd Amendment (1976).
VThe Union52–151President, Vice-President, Council of Ministers, Parliament, Supreme Court, CAG.
VIThe States152–237Governor, Council of Ministers, State Legislature, High Courts, Subordinate Courts.
VIIStates in Part B (Repealed)238Repealed by the 7th Amendment Act, 1956.
VIIIUnion Territories239–242Administration of Union Territories by the President.
IXThe Panchayats243–243OConstitutional status to Panchayati Raj. Added by 73rd Amendment (1992).
IXAThe Municipalities243P–243ZGConstitutional status to urban local bodies. Added by 74th Amendment (1992).
IXBCo-operative Societies243ZH–243ZTDemocratic functioning of co-operatives. Added by 97th Amendment (2011).
XScheduled and Tribal Areas244–244AAdministration of Scheduled Areas under Fifth and Sixth Schedules.
XIRelations between Union and States245–263Legislative and administrative relations, distribution of legislative powers.
XIIFinance, Property, Contracts, Suits264–300ADistribution of revenues, Finance Commission, right to property.
XIIITrade, Commerce and Intercourse301–307Freedom of trade and commerce within India.
XIVServices under Union and States308–323Public Service Commissions (UPSC, State PSCs), civil service safeguards.
XIVATribunals323A–323BAdministrative and other tribunals. Added by 42nd Amendment (1976).
XVElections324–329AElection Commission, adult suffrage, electoral rolls.
XVISpecial Provisions for Certain Classes330–342Reservations for SCs, STs, OBCs. National Commissions.
XVIIOfficial Language343–351Hindi as official language, use of English, regional languages.
XVIIIEmergency Provisions352–360National Emergency, President's Rule, Financial Emergency.
XIXMiscellaneous361–367Protection of President/Governors, rights of legislators.
XXAmendment of the Constitution368Procedure for amending the Constitution.
XXITemporary, Transitional and Special Provisions369–392Special provisions for states, Article 370 (now abrogated).
XXIIShort Title, Commencement, Repeals393–395Official name, date of commencement, repeal of earlier acts.
